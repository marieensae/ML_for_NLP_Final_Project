This study presents a computational analysis of over 8,000 electoral manifestos
from the French legislative elections of 1981 and 1988, archived in the ARCH-
ELEC database maintained by Sciences Po. Given the impossibility of exhaustive
manual reading at this scale, we apply Natural Language Processing (NLP) tools
to extract dominant themes and map partisan positioning. Our pipeline combines
BERTopic, built on contextual sentence embeddings (SBERT), with UMAP for
two-dimensional projection, and selects the optimal number of topics via the Cv
coherence score. The results reveal markedly different thematic structures across
the two elections. In 1981, seven balanced topics capture a diversified political
landscape encompassing labor, ecology, and programmatic themes. In 1988, a
single dominant topic absorbs the vast majority of candidates, reflecting a dramatic
compression of electoral vocabulary around a left-right opposition axis. We further
observe the complete disappearance of ecology from the 1988 thematic structure,
a partial convergence of governing party discourses, and the growing discursive
marginalization of the Communist Party, confined to a niche topic centered on
wage demands and legislative action.
